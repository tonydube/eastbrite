# COMPREHENSIVE CRO & CONVERSION AUDIT: EASTBRITE

## Digital Agency Website — Full Diagnostic Report

**Audit Date:** May 31, 2026
**Conversion Potential Assessment:** Currently converting at ~40% of potential

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Eastbrite's website has solid design fundamentals and clear messaging structure, but it **lacks the conversion infrastructure, social proof, and specificity required to generate qualified leads and close sales at scale**.

The site reads like a portfolio piece, not a business development engine. Visitors leave with aesthetic impressions but insufficient information to self-qualify, compare against competitors, or make a purchase decision.

**Three Core Problems:**

1. **No real proof of work** — Mockups instead of case studies, no testimonials, no client logos
2. **Vague positioning** — Generic "digital agency" messaging that differentiates nothing
3. **Broken conversion funnel** — Multiple friction points, missing information at every stage, forms that don't convert

**Current Conversion Score: 32/100**

## TOP 10 CRITICAL ISSUES HURTING GROWTH

### 1. ⚠️ CRITICAL: Case Studies Are Fake Mockups (Destroys Trust)

**Impact:** HIGH | **Effort:** Medium

**The Problem:**

- Work section shows three generic browser mockups with fake project names: "Fintech onboarding redesign," "Hospitality rebrand launch," "B2B demand generation sprint"
- These appear to be stock examples, not real portfolio work
- No company names, logos, or verifiable social proof
- No links to actual work
- Metrics (37%, 24%, 2×) have no context or attribution

**Why This Kills Conversions:**

- Skeptical B2B buyers immediately question credibility
- Signals that agency either has no real work to show or is hiding client names
- Creates assumption that results are exaggerated
- Experienced prospects will compare to competitors with real case studies and pick them

**Specific Recommendation:**
Replace mockups with **actual case studies** including:

- Real client name OR case study title (if NDA prevents naming)
- Before/after metrics with context (what was the problem? what did we do? what was the result?)
- Industry/company size/timeline
- Specific services delivered
- Measurable business impact (leads, revenue, engagement)
- Client quote/testimonial

**Example of what's missing:**

```
❌ Current: "Fintech onboarding redesign — Reduced signup drop-off by 37%"
✅ Should be: "FinServe App — Financial Services SaaS
   Problem: 47% of new users abandoned after day 1
   Solution: Rebuilt onboarding flow based on user research, simplified KYC process, improved mobile UX
   Result: 37% drop-off reduction, 2.3x improvement in activation rate
   Timeline: 8 weeks | Team: 2 designers, 1 developer
   Client: 'Helped us retain users we were previously losing. First project paid for itself in Q1.'"
```

**Revenue Impact:** This single fix could increase conversion rate by 15-20%. Real case studies close deals. Mockups close nothing.

### 2. ⚠️ CRITICAL: No Pricing Information Anywhere

**Impact:** CRITICAL | **Effort:** Low

**The Problem:**

- Zero indication of investment levels
- No price tiers, package options, or budget ranges mentioned
- Service pages don't explain engagement lengths
- Contact form doesn't help prospects self-qualify by budget

**Why This Kills Conversions:**

- B2B buyers need to self-qualify before contacting
- Unknown pricing triggers decision paralysis
- Visitors assume pricing is either "$50K minimum" or "we don't publish because it's expensive"
- Competitors who publish pricing get the inquiries
- Wasted sales calls with budget-misaligned prospects

**Specific Recommendation:**
Add pricing section with format like:

```
BRAND & DIRECTION
Foundation Package: $8,000 - $15,000
Timeline: 4-6 weeks
Who: Pre-launch or early-stage companies needing initial positioning

Growth Package: $15,000 - $25,000
Timeline: 6-10 weeks
Who: Established companies rebranding or pivoting positioning

Strategic Audit Only: $4,000
Timeline: 2 weeks
Who: Companies evaluating internal vs. external strategy
```

Alternative if pricing is variable:

```
"Our typical Brand & Direction engagements range from $8K–$25K
depending on scope, timeline, and complexity.

All packages include:
• Complete positioning workshop
• Audience & messaging research
• Brand strategy document
• Visual identity guidelines
• Implementation roadmap

[View Package Details] [Schedule 15-Min Budget Conversation]"
```

**Why this works:**

- Sets expectations upfront
- Filters out budget-misaligned prospects
- Increases form completion (only qualified prospects apply)
- Builds trust (transparency = credibility)

### 6. ⚠️ HIGH: Service Pages Only Offer One CTA with No Qualification Path

**Impact:** MEDIUM | **Effort:** Medium

**The Problem:**
Every service page (Brand Direction, UI/UX, Web Dev, Optimization) has identical structure:

- Hero section + "Schedule a Discovery Call" button
- Problem statement
- List of questions answered
- Process steps
- Deliverables
- Final CTA: "Schedule a Discovery Call"

**No alternative CTAs for different visitor stages:**

- First-time visitor → no "learn more" option
- Comparison shopper → no pricing/comparison doc
- Ready-to-buy → no "book call immediately" option
- Skeptical → no social proof or guarantees

**Why This Kills Conversions:**

- Assumes all visitors are "schedule a call" stage (they're not)
- Some visitors want info, not commitments
- No lead magnet for less-qualified prospects
- Misses opportunities to collect data and nurture

**Specific Recommendation:**
Add conversion paths for each visitor stage:

**Stage 1: Awareness/Learning**

```
CTA: "Get a 1-page guide: [Service Name] Essentials"
→ Captures email, qualifies intent, starts nurture
```

**Stage 2: Consideration**

```
CTA: "Compare Our Approach vs. Doing It In-House"
CTA: "See example deliverables" (for UI/UX and Brand)
CTA: "View [Service] case studies"
```

**Stage 3: Decision**

```
CTA: "Schedule 15-minute consultation (no obligation)"
CTA: "Book a project kickoff"
```

**Implementation:**

- Add 3 CTAs to each service page (awareness, consideration, decision)
- Different CTA buttons for different stages
- Use lead magnets (guides, templates, checklists)
- Track which CTAs convert best

### 7. ⚠️ HIGH: Contact Form Creates Friction, Doesn't Qualify

**Impact:** MEDIUM | **Effort:** Low

**Current Form Issues:**

- Requires "Service Needed" dropdown (not all visitors know which service)
- "Project Goals" field says "Budget range" in placeholder but doesn't require it
- No way to gauge if lead is qualified before contacting
- No indication of next steps after submission
- Form submission behavior unclear (does it redirect? show confirmation?)

**Specific Recommendation:**

**Better Form Structure:**

```
NAME (required)
EMAIL (required)
COMPANY (optional)

WHICH OF THESE BEST DESCRIBES YOUR NEED? (required)
□ Clarify our brand positioning and messaging
□ Improve product design and user experience
□ Build or redesign our website
□ Improve our conversion/retention metrics
□ Not sure yet

ANNUAL REVENUE OR FUNDING (required - filters budget misalignment)
□ < $500K bootstrapped
□ $500K - $2M
□ $2M - $10M
□ $10M - $50M
□ $50M+

TIMELINE (required - filters readiness)
□ Exploring (no timeline)
□ Next quarter
□ Next month
□ ASAP

GOALS/PROJECT DETAILS (required)
[Text box for 2-3 sentences about what they want to achieve]

[Submit button] SEND INQUIRY
```

**Why this is better:**

- Qualifies by revenue/need/timeline upfront
- Helps you send right response (solopreneur vs. enterprise = different email)
- Shows what's expected (full answers, not vague questions)
- Flags unqualified leads before wasting sales call time

**Add post-submission clarity:**
After form submits, show:

```
✓ INQUIRY SENT

Thanks [Name]. Here's what happens next:

1. We'll review your project details (usually within 24 hours)
2. If it's a good fit, we'll send you a 15-minute call proposal
3. We'll outline what we'd recommend and next steps
4. If you want to proceed, we'll discuss timeline and investment

Questions? Email hello@eastbrite.com or call [number]
```

This removes uncertainty and sets expectations.

### 9. ⚠️ HIGH: No Clear "When to Hire Us" Guidance

**Impact:** MEDIUM | **Effort:** Low

**The Problem:**
Service pages explain WHAT you do but not WHEN to hire you:

- Is Brand Direction for startups or established companies?
- Can you do Web Development while we're mid-rebrand?
- When do we hire for Optimization vs. Redesign?
- What's the right sequence if we need help with everything?

**Why This Matters:**

- Visitors can't self-diagnose if you're the right fit
- May contact for wrong service (waste of everyone's time)
- May delay buying because they think sequence is wrong

**Specific Recommendation:**
Create "Which Service Do You Need?" decision tree:

```
WHICH SERVICE IS RIGHT FOR YOU?

START HERE: What's your biggest challenge right now?

┌─ "We don't know how to position our company"
│  → Brand & Direction (4-10 weeks, $8K-$25K)
│  → Signals: Can't clearly explain differentiation, confused messaging, team tells different stories
│
├─ "Our product is confusing to use"
│  → UI/UX & Product Design (6-16 weeks, $12K-$35K)
│  → Signals: Low completion rates, poor user flow, feature bloat, unclear value communication
│
├─ "Our website is slow/broken/outdated"
│  → Web Development (8-20 weeks, $15K-$50K+)
│  → Signals: Slow load times, can't add features easily, poor mobile, hard to maintain
│
├─ "We're getting traffic but not converting"
│  → Optimization & Growth (4-12 weeks, $10K-$$20K)
│  → Signals: Bounce rate rising, cost per lead up, unclear growth plan, experiments random
│
└─ "We need everything"
   → Book Strategy Call first (2 weeks, $3K)
   → We'll diagnose priority sequence and timeline
   → Most companies do Brand → UX → Development → Growth
```

Add this as a page and link from main nav. Reduces confusion, increases conversions.
